Child Vaccination Resources

Here you can find information about child vaccinations by age group.

Birth to 1 Year:

  • BCG (Bacillus Calmette-Gu√©rin): Given at birth or till 1 year of age, it provides protection against tuberculosis.
  • Hepatitis B: Given at birth and it provides protection against hepatitis B i.e liver affecting virus.It's protection last for 20 years.
  • OPV (Oral Polio Vaccine): Given at birth, 6, 10 and 14 weeks, and administered in multiple doses to protect against polio.
  • DPT (Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus): Administered in multiple doses, typically at 6, 10, and 14 weeks of age.
  • Hib (Haemophilus influenzae type b): Given at 6, 10, and 14 weeks to protect against diseases caused by Haemophilus influenzae type b.
  • IPV (Inactivated Polio Vaccine): Given at 14 weeks as an additional protection against polio.
  • PCV (Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine): Given at 6, 10, and 14 weeks to protect against pneumococcal diseases.

1 to 5 Years:

  • MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella):Given at 9-12 months of age, with a booster dose at 16-24 months.
  • Varicella (Chickenpox): Administered at 15 months of age.
  • DTaP (Diphtheria, Tetanus, acellular Pertussis): Given as a booster dose at 18 months.
  • OPV (Oral Polio Vaccine): Booster doses at 18 months and 5 years.

5 to 10 Years:

  • TT (Tetanus Toxoid):A booster dose is given between 10-16 years of age.
  • HPV (Human Papillomavirus) Vaccine: Recommended for girls between 9-14 years for protection against cervical cancer. The number of doses may vary depending on the specific vaccine used.
